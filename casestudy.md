## Case Study: "The Wrong Direction" — A UPI Collect Request Scam

### Background

Riya, a 19-year-old first-year engineering student in Indore, posted in a college WhatsApp group that she was selling her old laptop for ₹18,000. Within an hour, she received a message from a number she did not recognise, claiming to be an interested buyer named "Ajay Verma."

### How It Happened – Step by Step

1. **Contact:** Ajay messaged Riya saying he was interested in buying the laptop and would pay immediately via UPI.

2. **Building trust:** He shared a fake screenshot of a ₹18,000 "payment sent" confirmation to make Riya believe money was already on the way.

3. **The trick:** Ajay then sent Riya a UPI "collect request" for ₹18,000 which is a request for her to send money to him — and told her, "You just need to approve this to receive your payment."

4. **The deception:** Riya, confused by the process, entered her UPI PIN to "confirm receipt." She had unknowingly approved a payment to him.

5. **Outcome:** ₹18,000 was debited from Riya's account. Ajay immediately blocked her. By the time she understood what had happened, the fraudster was unreachable.

### Who Is Targeted

Young adults, students, and first-time digital payment users anyone unfamiliar with the difference between a UPI payment link (to receive money) and a UPI collect request (to send money).

### Consequences

Financial loss (often unrecoverable), emotional distress, and loss of trust in digital payment systems. In India, UPI reversals are not guaranteed in fraud cases, and recovery through banks is slow and uncertain.
