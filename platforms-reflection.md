# Task 3 – Coding & Collaboration Platforms

## Part A – Coding Practice: HackerRank

**Platform used:** HackerRank (hackerrank.com)

I created an account on HackerRank and completed the **"Solve Me First"** challenge under the Algorithms section: a beginner-level problem that asks you to read two integers and print their sum. Although the problem itself is straightforward, the process of navigating the platform, reading the problem statement, selecting a language (Python 3), writing the solution in the browser-based editor, and submitting it was a valuable first experience with a competitive coding environment.

**Challenge completed:** Solve Me First  
**Language used:** Python 3  
**Result:** Accepted (all test cases passed)

## Part B – Google Workspace Collaboration: Google Forms

**Tool used:** Google Forms (forms.google.com)

I created a **Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz** with 5 questions designed to test basic digital literacy knowledge among first-year students. The form includes:

1. *(Multiple Choice)* What does the padlock icon in a browser's address bar indicate?
2. *(Multiple Choice)* Which of the following is considered a strong password?
3. *(Multiple Choice)* What is phishing?
4. *(Short Answer)* Name one platform where students can practice coding online.
5. *(Short Answer)* What steps would you take if you received a suspicious email from an unknown sender?

The form is linked to a Google Sheet that automatically collects and organises responses. This makes it easy to review answers and analyse how much students already know about digital safety.

🔗 **Quiz Link:** _[https://forms.gle/JtmdnZmyCSrJdMkz7]_

## Reflection

HackerRank was more approachable than I expected. The interface is clean and it gives immediate feedback - when my code passed all test cases, it was genuinely satisfying. I plan to continue solving problems on HackerRank to build problem-solving skills alongside my coursework. Google Forms was something I had used before to fill out surveys, but building one from scratch, especially thinking about question types and how the responses link to a spreadsheet, was a new and practical experience. These tools will help me both academically (organising data, practising coding) and in collaborative projects where real-time sharing and feedback are important.
